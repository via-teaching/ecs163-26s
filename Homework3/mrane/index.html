University Student Mental Health Dashboard

This dashboard explores student mental health in terms of its correlation to gender, year of study, GPA, and condition type. For an overview of the population and relevant variables, observe the Sankey diagram on the left, checking ribbon size to see how students flow from gender and year variables into condition and treatment status. To explore the relationship between certain variables and conditions better, click-select the relevant bar of the variable on the Sankey diagram (e.g. Year 1, Has Condition, etc.). Then, examine whether GPA ranges for that variable seem to be correlating condition rates using the bar chart on the top right, which will be automatically updated through an animation according to your selection. Finally, compare how Depression, Anxiety, and Panic Attacks fluctuate for students in every year who identify with that variable using the heatmap which will also update on the bottom right. Hovering over data in each of these graphs will show you the exact number of students that the percentages have been derived from. In addition, if you brush a range of CGPAs of the bar chart, those values will be reflected in the heatmap as well, so you can assess the patterns in condition prevelance and year of study for groups with specific CPAs.
