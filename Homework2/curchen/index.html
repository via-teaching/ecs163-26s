What Makes a Pokemon Rare?

Three lenses on Pokemon rarity: by breeding (who cannot reproduce), by type identity (which combinations are vanishingly rare), and by catchability (the legendaries you can barely catch).

Overview. Rarity by Breeding: Who Cannot Reproduce?

Every Pokemon belongs to an egg group. Arc thickness shows group population; chord thickness shows Pokemon shared by both groups. Undiscovered (legendaries, mythicals, babies) sits isolated with no chords. Its members cannot breed at all, which means you can only ever obtain a single copy of each one, making them very rare.

Rarity by Type: Common Archetypes vs. Vanishing Combos

Cell darkness shows how many Pokemon share that primary and secondary type pairing. Diagonal cells are mono-type Pokemon (only one type, such as Normal mono-type or Water mono-type). Pale blue cells are the rare combinations, often where legendaries live. Empty cells mean no Pokemon belong to that type pairing at all.

The Cost of Rarity: Power vs. Catchability

Each dot is one Pokemon. The dots in the very top left tend to be Undiscovered (legendaries, mythicals, babies) and have very high Base Stat Totals. The reason they are rare is because their high base stats make them very strong, so the game balances them out by making them extremely hard to catch.
